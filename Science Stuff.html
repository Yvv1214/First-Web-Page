DNA is the blueprint of our bodies. The order of the genomes determines our characteristics: Phenotypes and Genotypes. Our DNA has something called telomeres and when it is damaged it causes "aging". That is becuase telomeres are like a coated cap end to our DNA. Without it our DNA starts to get frayed meaning that our "blueprint" or our bodies "manual" starts getting missing "instructions" or functions. Our telomeres have a protective enzyme called the telomerase, kit counteracts the damage to telomeres. But as cells divide repeatedly, there seems to be less telomerase, therefor the damage to telomeres begin and biological aging beigns. There are ways to slow this process down with HIIT training, fruits high in antioxidants, sleep to detox our lymph nodes, and collagen rich foods. Telomerase can reverse the aging process, but it is a very complex issue becuase at one point of our lifes our bodies hit an off switch for telomerase and introducing the enzymes that encourage production of telomerase is linked to cancer. This becuase cancer cells are abnormal cells that use telomerase to maintain themselves while normal cells are programmed to self destruct. We can't use cancer cells to replenish our telomerase because they destroy our healthy cells we need to live and they divide uncontrollably due to the absence of P50 if I remember correctly.
